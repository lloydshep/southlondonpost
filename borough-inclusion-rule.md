# Borough inclusion rule

## The rule

A London borough is included in this project if and only if it satisfies **both** of the following criteria:

1. It is one of the **twelve statutory Inner London boroughs** as defined by Schedule 1 of the [London Government Act 1963 (c.33)](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1963/33/schedule/1).
2. It lies **south of the River Thames**.

## Resulting boroughs

Applying these two criteria produces exactly five boroughs:

| Borough | Inner London (1963 Act) | South of Thames |
|---|---|---|
| London Borough of Lambeth | ✓ | ✓ |
| London Borough of Southwark | ✓ | ✓ |
| London Borough of Lewisham | ✓ | ✓ |
| London Borough of Wandsworth | ✓ | ✓ |
| Royal Borough of Greenwich | ✓ | ✓ |

## Explicit exclusions

### Outer London boroughs (south of Thames, but not Inner London under the 1963 Act)

Croydon, Bromley, Merton, Sutton, Bexley, Kingston upon Thames, and Richmond upon Thames are all south of the Thames but were designated **Outer London boroughs** by the 1963 Act and are therefore outside scope.

### Inner London boroughs north of the Thames

Camden, Hackney, Hammersmith and Fulham, Islington, Kensington and Chelsea, Tower Hamlets, and Westminster are statutory Inner London boroughs but lie north of the Thames and are therefore outside scope.

### Richmond upon Thames

Richmond upon Thames straddles both banks of the Thames but is classified as an **Outer London borough** under the 1963 Act and is therefore outside scope regardless of its partial southern extent.

## Edge case: Greenwich

The Royal Borough of Greenwich has a small northern section that touches the Thames at East Greenwich. However, the borough as a whole is unambiguously classified as a south-of-Thames Inner London borough in all authoritative sources and presents no practical difficulty.

## Alternatives considered and rejected

### Boundary Commission for England 2011 grouping (12 boroughs)

The Boundary Commission's 2011 parliamentary grouping includes all 12 south-of-Thames boroughs: Bexley, Bromley, Croydon, Greenwich, Kingston, Lambeth, Lewisham, Merton, Richmond, Southwark, Sutton, and Wandsworth.

**Rejected because:** it includes Outer London boroughs (Croydon, Bromley, Merton, Sutton, Bexley, Kingston, Richmond), significantly extending the geographic scope beyond the inner-city focus of this service.

### GLA South West sub-region (London Plan 2008, 7 boroughs)

Croydon, Lambeth, Merton, Sutton, Kingston, Richmond, Wandsworth.

**Rejected because:** it excludes Southwark, Lewisham, and Greenwich — placing Peckham, Deptford, and Greenwich outside "South London", which contradicts common usage and editorial intent.

### GLA South East sub-region (London Plan 2008, 5 boroughs)

Southwark, Lewisham, Greenwich, Bexley, Bromley.

**Rejected because:** it excludes Lambeth and Wandsworth, omitting Brixton, Clapham, Tooting, and Battersea — again contradicting common usage.

### River Thames boundary (popular convention, ~12 boroughs)

Any borough whose land lies primarily south of the Thames.

**Rejected because:** it has no statutory basis and cannot be cited to a single authoritative source.
